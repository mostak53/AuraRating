Science of Beauty

The Golden Ratio
& Human Faces

The Golden Ratio (φ ≈ 1.618) has fascinated mathematicians for millennia. Studies suggest faces whose proportions approximate this ratio are consistently perceived as more attractive across cultures.

φ

Phi Ratio (1.618)

The ideal ratio present in facial symmetry — forehead to chin, nose width to mouth width.

72%

Symmetry Factor

Research shows humans are 72% more likely to rate symmetrical faces as attractive regardless of other features.

5

Core Proportions

Five key ratios: eye spacing, nose length, mouth width, jaw width, and forehead height.

Who We Are

About This Project

AuraScore is an academic research project studying human aesthetic perception. By collecting crowd-sourced ratings, we aim to understand how facial features, angles, and proportions are perceived across diverse participant groups.

All data is anonymized. Participants may withdraw at any time. This project is conducted in accordance with ethical research standards.
